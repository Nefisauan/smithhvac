AC repair in Utah typically ranges from $150 to $500 for most common issues like capacitor replacement, refrigerant recharge, or electrical repairs. More complex issues like compressor replacement can run $800–$1,500. We always give you upfront pricing before starting — no surprises.

A common rule is the 5,000 rule: multiply the system's age (in years) by the repair cost. If the number exceeds $5,000, replacement is likely the better investment. Also consider: if your system is 15+ years old, if you're facing major component failure, or if energy bills are rising significantly.

Yes! We offer same-day service for most calls and a 24/7 emergency line for urgent situations. We understand that when your heat goes out in January or your AC fails in July, you can't afford to wait. Call (801) 735-4577 anytime.

We recommend twice a year — once in spring for your AC before summer, and once in fall for your furnace before winter. Annual maintenance keeps your system running efficiently, extends its lifespan, and helps catch small issues before they become expensive repairs.

Standard 1-inch filters should be changed every 1–3 months. Thicker 4-inch media filters can last 6–12 months. If you have pets, allergies, or dusty conditions, change more frequently. A clogged filter is the #1 cause of HVAC problems and can even cause system failure.

Radon is a naturally occurring radioactive gas that seeps up from soil and rock. Utah has higher-than-average radon levels due to its geology. It's colorless, odorless, and is the #2 cause of lung cancer in the US. We offer professional radon testing (starting at $150) and mitigation systems to protect your family.
